AC Blowing Warm Air: Professional HVAC Diagnostic Guide

AC blowing warm air is most often caused by low refrigerant or restricted airflow. Check the air filter first, then follow the diagnostic flow below to isolate the problem.

Low Refrigerant Levels Refrigerant leaks reduce cooling capacity and cause the evaporator coil to warm. Check for ice on the coil and measure system pressure.

Common Mistakes

  • ✗ Replacing the compressor before checking refrigerant
  • ✗ Ignoring dirty filters
  • ✗ Handling refrigerant without EPA certification

Prevention Tips

  • • Replace filter monthly during heavy use
  • • Clean condenser coils annually
  • • Schedule annual professional maintenance

When to Call an HVAC Technician

  • 1. Electrical Risk: Contactors, capacitors, control boards require LOTO training.
  • 2. Refrigerant: EPA Section 608—illegal to vent or handle without license.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is my AC blowing warm air?

Most often low refrigerant or restricted airflow. Check the filter first.

Can a dirty filter cause AC to stop cooling?

Yes. A clogged filter restricts airflow and can cause the evaporator to freeze.
